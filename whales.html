Minkes are the only baleen whale species which is still common in Antarctic waters and apparently the most ice adapted of the Antarctic baleen whales. Minkes are regarded as very inquisitive animals. They will often swim repeatedly around a small vessel, and go out of their way to approach a moving ship, before veering away at high speed.

Humpback Whale

STATUS

Least Concern

DIET

Krill, small fish

Humpback whales, unlike their ‘cousins’ the blue, sei and minke whales, are not slender and graceful—they tend to be quite stout and stocky, with exceedingly long pectoral fins (their ‘arms’). They are much slower swimmers than these other species. Humpbacks are known to have the longest annual migration of any mammal.

Blue Whale

STATUS

Endangered

DIET

Krill

The biggest blue whale ever weighed was nearly 200 tonnes and over 30 m in length. Blue whales are one of the fussiest eaters in the Antarctic, usually eating only Antarctic krill. Low frequency moans of blue whales can theoretically travel several thousand kilometres, and at close range are as loud as a jumbo jet taking off.
